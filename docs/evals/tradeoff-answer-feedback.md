# Tradeoff Answer Feedback

These lightweight eval targets come from retained model-run feedback. They are not an automated eval suite yet; use them when reviewing runtime-doctrine or prompt changes.

## Speed work vs mileage with shin pain

Question:

> Is it better to reduce speed work and preserve mileage or sacrifice mileage for speedwork?

Feedback signal:

> Helpful, but too abstract. The answer should make the costs and benefits tangible: missing a speed session or two over the next two weeks costs something and buys something; sacrificing mileage costs and buys something else; each path carries a different risk profile.

Desired behavior:

- Make a clear recommendation first.
- Explain what the recommended choice buys.
- Explain what the recommended choice costs.
- Explain what the rejected alternative buys and costs.
- Name the risk difference between the paths.
- Give a practical signal that would change the recommendation.

Example target shape:

> I would reduce speed work and preserve easy mileage for the next one to two weeks. That buys lower shin-load risk while keeping aerobic momentum, and the cost is only a small delay in sharpening because there is no near-term race. Sacrificing mileage for speedwork buys a cleaner quality stimulus, but it concentrates load around the exact thing that may be provoking the shin. I would bring speed back once the shin is quiet for several easy runs and no longer tender afterward.
